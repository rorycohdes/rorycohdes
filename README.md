Student, Digital Craftsman
--------------------------

I first started coding in high school with pascal it felt natural as if I had a coding fiber. Coding to me provides a world of opportunities so when I realized that I could use coding as a way to fund my university study abroad I decided to take it seriously and started my coding journey with something that is low friction and I knew I could get into easily without being scared off by the intricacies of coding. Everyone loves games including myself and so I delved into unity and started working on small platformer games. The ultimate goal was to create a viral hyper casual game using unique concepts. I ended up creating projects for fun instead of profit. Fast forward to my final your in high school my prospects at university continued to loom over my head. So I took a gap year after university to work on my “moonshot” idea. Through this endeavor I taught myself JavaScript and its frameworks as it suited the objectives of my project. I created over a dozen small project to prime my skills for the project that would ultimately become my business. I felt like a black sheep, however, I pushed through. Fortunately for me I was blessed with the opportunity to study abroad and I ceased it. Now I am studying in the US and working on my project on the side and perpetually learning new technologies. The Indiehacker journey continues. My WHY has change to creating solutions that create convenience for people even while studying as student in my youth. “There is no passion to be found playing small - in settling for a life that is less than the one you are capable of living.” - Nelson Mandela
